# Methodology Critique

## Overview

Evaluate research methodology for rigor, validity, and potential flaws. This guide provides a systematic framework for assessing whether study designs can support the claims being made.

## When to Apply

- Reviewing research papers
- Assessing experimental designs
- Evaluating study protocols
- Planning new research

## Evaluation Framework

### 1. Study Design Assessment

**Questions to ask:**
- Is the design appropriate for the research question?
- Can the design support causal claims being made?
- Are comparison groups appropriate and adequate?
- Is experimental, quasi-experimental, or observational design justified?

**What to look for:**
- Clear alignment between research question and design choice
- Appropriate use of randomization for causal claims
- Valid control or comparison groups
- Justification for design limitations

### 2. Validity Analysis

#### Internal Validity: Can we trust the causal inference?

**Check for:**
- **Randomization quality**: Was randomization properly implemented?
- **Confounding control**: Are confounders identified and controlled?
- **Selection bias**: Could sample selection affect results?
- **Attrition/dropout patterns**: Is dropout differential between groups?

**Red flags:**
- High or differential dropout rates
- Baseline differences between groups
- Unclear randomization procedures
- Unmeasured confounders

#### External Validity: Do results generalize?

**Evaluate:**
- **Sample representativeness**: Does sample represent target population?
- **Ecological validity**: Do conditions match target application?
- **Setting generalizability**: Can results transfer to other settings?

**Red flags:**
- Highly selected samples (volunteers, convenience sampling)
- Artificial laboratory conditions for real-world claims
- Single-site studies claiming broad generalization

#### Construct Validity: Do measures capture intended constructs?

**Review:**
- **Measurement validation**: Are instruments validated?
- **Operational definitions**: Are constructs clearly defined?
- **Direct vs. proxy measures**: Are measures appropriate?

**Red flags:**
- Unvalidated or ad-hoc measures
- Vague construct definitions
- Proxy measures for critical outcomes

#### Statistical Conclusion Validity: Are statistical inferences sound?

**Verify:**
- **Adequate power/sample size**: Is the study powered appropriately?
- **Assumption compliance**: Are statistical assumptions met?
- **Test appropriateness**: Are tests suitable for data and design?

**Red flags:**
- No power analysis or underpowered studies
- Violations of statistical assumptions
- Inappropriate statistical tests

### 3. Control and Blinding

**Assess:**
- **Randomization implementation**: Sequence generation and allocation concealment
- **Blinding feasibility**: Was blinding possible and implemented?
  - Participants blinded?
  - Providers blinded?
  - Assessors blinded?
- **Control conditions**: Are controls appropriate (placebo, active control, no treatment)?
- **Bias potential**: Could performance or detection bias affect results?

**Best practices:**
- Double-blind when possible
- Single-blind if double-blind not feasible
- Clear documentation of who was blinded
- Blinding integrity checks

### 4. Measurement Quality

**Evaluate:**
- **Instrument validation**: Are instruments validated and reliable?
- **Measurement objectivity**: Objective when possible, subjective with acknowledged limitations?
- **Standardization**: Is outcome assessment standardized?
- **Triangulation**: Are multiple measures used?

**Quality indicators:**
- Published validation studies
- High reliability coefficients (e.g., α > 0.70)
- Standardized protocols
- Multiple measurement methods

## Common Issues to Identify

### Critical Issues (threaten validity of main conclusions)
- Fundamental design-question mismatch
- Major confounding not addressed
- Invalid measurement of primary outcome
- Severe selection bias
- Massive attrition (>40%)

### Important Issues (affect interpretation but not fatally)
- Limited generalizability
- Some confounding not controlled
- Moderate attrition (20-40%)
- Weak validation of some measures

### Minor Issues (worth noting but don't change conclusions)
- Small sample from power perspective but adequate for effect
- Some unclear methodological details
- Minor protocol deviations

## Providing Feedback

**Structure your critique:**

1. **Design Appropriateness**
   - "The [design type] is appropriate/inappropriate for the research question because..."
   - "The design supports/does not support the causal claims because..."

2. **Validity Concerns**
   - "Internal validity is threatened by [specific issue]..."
   - "Generalization is limited because [specific limitation]..."
   - "Measurement validity concerns include [specific issues]..."

3. **Strengths**
   - "Strong aspects of the methodology include..."
   - "The study appropriately addresses [specific concern]..."

4. **Recommendations**
   - "To strengthen internal validity, consider..."
   - "Generalizability could be improved by..."
   - "Measurement could be enhanced by..."
